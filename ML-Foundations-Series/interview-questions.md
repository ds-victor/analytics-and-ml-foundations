# Machine Learning Foundations Interview Questions - Comprehensive Pool

This pool contains 60+ questions to help you prepare for entry-level Machine Learning roles.

---

## 1. Technical Vertical (20 Questions)

### Easy
1. **Definition:** What is Machine Learning?
2. **Paradigms:** Explain the difference between Supervised and Unsupervised learning.
3. **Regression:** What are you trying to predict in a regression task?
4. **Classification:** What is the difference between binary and multi-class classification?
5. **Data:** What is the purpose of a 'Target' variable?
6. **Features:** What is a 'Feature' in the context of ML?
7. **Splitting:** Why do we split data into training and testing sets?

### Medium
8. **Evaluation:** Explain 'Mean Squared Error (MSE)' and 'Mean Absolute Error (MAE)'.
9. **Overfitting:** What are the signs of an overfit model and how do you fix it?
10. **Algorithms:** How does a 'Decision Tree' work?
11. **Validation:** What is 'K-fold Cross-Validation' and why is it used?
12. **Bias-Variance:** Explain the 'Bias-Variance Trade-off' in simple terms.
13. **Metrics:** When would you use 'Precision' and 'Recall' instead of 'Accuracy'?
14. **Categorical Data:** What is 'One-Hot Encoding' and why is it necessary?

### Difficult
15. **Ensembles:** Explain how a 'Random Forest' improves upon a single Decision Tree.
16. **Regularization:** What is 'L1' and 'L2' regularization?
17. **Imbalanced Data:** How would you evaluate a model trained on a highly imbalanced dataset?
18. **Math:** Explain the concept of 'Gradient Descent' at a high level.
19. **Optimization:** What is the difference between 'Hyperparameters' and 'Model Parameters'?
20. **Loss Functions:** Why do we minimize a loss function during training?

## 2. Situational Vertical (20 Questions)

### Easy
21. Your model's accuracy is 99% on the training set but 50% on the test set. What do you do?
22. You have a very small dataset (100 rows). Which algorithm would you try first?
23. A stakeholder asks you to "just use AI" for a problem that can be solved with a simple rule. How do you respond?
24. You find that your dataset has many missing values. What are your first steps?
25. Your code runs locally but crashes on the server. How do you troubleshoot?
26. A manager asks for a model that is "100% accurate." How do you manage their expectations?

### Medium
27. You notice that one feature is highly correlated with the target variable. Is this a good thing? What do you check?
28. After deployment, your model's performance starts to drop. How do you identify the cause?
29. A domain expert says your model's top feature "doesn't make sense." How do you handle this?
30. You have to choose between a simple Linear Regression and a complex Neural Network. What factors influence your decision?
31. Your model is taking too long to train. What are three things you could try to speed it up?
32. You are asked to build a recommendation system but have no historical user data. How do you start?
33. Your test set accuracy is much lower than you expected. How do you decide if the problem is the model or the data?

### Difficult
34. You discovered 'Data Leakage' in your pipeline after the model was built. How do you go about fixing it?
35. Your model is showing bias against a specific demographic group. What technical steps do you take to mitigate this?
36. You have a budget for only one of the following: more data, more features, or more compute. Which do you pick?
37. A stakeholder wants an 'interpretable' model, but your best performer is a 'black box.' How do you compromise?
38. You are working with time-series data. How does your train-test split strategy change?
39. You found that your features have very different scales. How does this affect a distance-based algorithm like K-Nearest Neighbors?
40. You need to deploy an ML model that must provide predictions in less than 10 milliseconds. How do you optimize for latency?

## 3. Behavioural Vertical (20 Questions)

### Easy
41. Tell me about an ML project you're particularly proud of.
42. How do you stay up-to-date with the latest ML research?
43. Describe a time you worked on a team project. What was your role?
44. Why did you choose to specialize in Machine Learning?
45. What is your favorite ML algorithm and why?
46. How do you handle failure when a model you built doesn't work?

### Medium
47. Tell me about a time you had to explain a complex ML concept to a non-technical stakeholder.
48. Describe a situation where you had to prioritize multiple ML projects at once.
49. Give an example of a time you received constructive feedback on your code.
50. Tell me about a time you had a disagreement with a teammate about a technical choice.
51. Describe a time you had to learn a new ML framework (like PyTorch or TensorFlow) quickly.
52. How do you balance the trade-off between model accuracy and business impact?
53. Describe a time you identified a problem in a dataset that others had missed.

### Difficult
54. Tell me about a time you had to make a difficult trade-off between technical excellence and a deadline.
55. Describe a situation where you had to lead an ML initiative from scratch.
56. Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager's technical direction.
57. What is the most challenging ML problem you've ever solved? Walk me through it.
58. Describe a time you had to handle an ethical concern related to data or AI.
59. How do you handle a situation where a stakeholder is putting pressure on you to "massage" the results?
60. Where do you see the field of Machine Learning heading in the next 5 years?
