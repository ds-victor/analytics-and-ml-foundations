## Explanations & Review

### Beginner
- **Q1 (Roles):** Data Science is inherently predictive.
- **Q2 (Scoping):** SMART stands for Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.
- **Q5 (EDA):** `location` contains names (categories), whereas others are numbers.

### Intermediate
- **Q7 (Metrics):** RMSE squares the errors, making large errors much more costly than small ones.
- **Q8 (Reproducibility):** Data leakage is a top reason why models fail in the real world despite high validation scores.
- **Q11 (Splits):** Stratification is key when you have imbalanced classes or important categories.

### Advanced
- **Q15 (Trade-offs):** High training accuracy + low test accuracy = the model "memorized" the training data (Overfitting).
- **Q16 (Leakage):** Any feature that uses the target variable MUST be calculated within the training set only, never using test data information.
- **Q19 (Splits):** Temporal splits use time to mimic the real-world scenario where you predict the future based on the past.
