# The Business Analyst Role

## 1. Why This Matters
Business analysts bridge the gap between business needs and data solutions. They don't need to code, but they must understand data.

## 2. Core Concept
A **Business Analyst** translates business problems into requirements, analyses data (often in Excel/Power BI), and recommends actions. They work with stakeholders, not just data.

## 3. Real-World Examples
• A BA at a retail chain identifies that discount timing should change based on inventory.
• A BA in finance analyses budget vs actuals and advises on cost cutting.

## 4. Comparison
| Aspect | Business Analyst | Data Analyst |
|--------|------------------|--------------|
| Focus | Business decisions | Data insights |
| Tools | Excel, Power BI, SQL (basic) | SQL, Python, Tableau |
| Deliverables | Reports, requirements, presentations | Dashboards, statistical analysis |
| Stakeholders | Executives, product managers | Data scientists, managers |

## 5. Decision Tree
1. Love strategy and people? → Business Analyst
2. Love building dashboards and queries? → Data Analyst
3. Love modelling and coding? → Data Scientist

## 6. Common Misconceptions
• Business analysts do need some technical skills – Excel and basic SQL are often required.
• They are not just 'note takers' – they drive decisions.

## 7. FAQ
**Q: Can I become a BA without a technical degree?** Yes, many BAs come from business, finance, or communications.
**Q: What's the career progression?** Senior BA → Product Manager → Director of Product.

## 9. Running Example
You are a Business Analyst for a real estate investment firm. Your job: use data to answer 'Should we invest more in luxury homes?' You'll define KPIs, analyse market trends, and present a recommendation to executives.

## 10. Interview Prep
1. How do you prioritise requirements from multiple stakeholders?
2. Tell me about a time you used data to influence a decision.
