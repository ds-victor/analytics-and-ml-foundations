# Roles & Domains for Data Analysts

## 1. Why This Matters
Understanding where data analysts fit helps you focus your learning. You'll work alongside data scientists and business analysts.

## 2. Core Concept
**Data Analyst**: answers business questions through data exploration, dashboards, and SQL. **Analytics Engineer**: builds data models for analysts (bridge between data engineering and analysis).

```mermaid
graph LR
    DE[Data Engineer] --> AE[Analytics Engineer]
    AE --> DA[Data Analyst]
    DA --> Stakeholder[Business Stakeholder]
```

## 3. Real-World Examples
• An e-commerce analyst tracks sales trends, customer segments, and conversion funnels.
• A finance analyst builds reports for quarterly earnings.

## 4. Comparison
| Role | Tools | Deliverables | Collaboration |
|------|-------|--------------|---------------|
| Data Analyst | SQL, Excel, Tableau | Dashboards, insights | Business teams |
| Analytics Engineer | dbt, SQL, Python | Transformed data | Data engineers |
| Business Analyst | Excel, Power BI | Requirements, decisions | Stakeholders |

## 5. Decision Tree
1. Love building dashboards and finding insights? → Data Analyst
2. Love preparing clean data for others? → Analytics Engineer
3. Love strategy and less technical work? → Business Analyst

## 6. Common Misconceptions
• Data Analysts do need to code – SQL is essential, Python is very helpful.
• You don't need to be a statistician, but basic stats is required.

## 7. FAQ
**Q: Can I become a data analyst without a degree?** Yes, many are self-taught with portfolios.
**Q: What's the career progression?** Senior Analyst → Analytics Manager → Head of Analytics.
