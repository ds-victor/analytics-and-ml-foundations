# Business Analyst Interview Questions - Comprehensive Pool

This pool contains 60+ questions to help you prepare for Business Analyst roles.

---

## 1. Technical Vertical (20 Questions)

### Easy
1. **Excel:** What is the difference between a relative and an absolute cell reference?
2. **KPIs:** What are the components of a SMART goal?
3. **Data Types:** Define the difference between qualitative and quantitative data.
4. **SQL:** What is the purpose of the `SELECT` statement?
5. **Documentation:** What is a 'User Story' and why is it used?
6. **Reporting:** Name three common types of charts and when to use them.
7. **Basic Math:** How do you calculate a percentage increase in sales?

### Medium
8. **Excel:** How does a `VLOOKUP` differ from an `INDEX-MATCH` combination?
9. **SQL:** Explain the difference between `GROUP BY` and `ORDER BY`.
10. **Requirements:** What is the difference between functional and non-functional requirements?
11. **BI Tools:** What is a 'Dimension' vs. a 'Measure' in Power BI or Tableau?
12. **Modeling:** What is a BPMN (Business Process Model and Notation) diagram used for?
13. **Data Quality:** How do you identify and handle duplicate records in a dataset?
14. **Stakeholders:** Describe the 'RACI' matrix and how it aids project management.

### Difficult
15. **Advanced SQL:** Write a query using a `Common Table Expression (CTE)` to calculate rolling 3-month average revenue.
16. **KPI Strategy:** If a primary KPI (like Revenue) is up, but a secondary one (like Profit Margin) is down, how do you investigate the root cause?
17. **Data Integration:** How would you approach merging data from two different CRM systems with inconsistent naming conventions?
18. **Statistical Insight:** Explain the concept of 'Correlation vs. Causation' using a real-world business example.
19. **Predictive Context:** How can a Business Analyst contribute to a machine learning project before the model is even built?
20. **ROI Analysis:** Walk through a Cost-Benefit Analysis for implementing a new automated reporting tool.

## 2. Situational Vertical (20 Questions)

### Easy
21. You are asked to create a report by EOD but the data source is down. What do you do?
22. A stakeholder asks for a chart that you think is misleading. How do you respond?
23. You find a minor error in a report you sent out an hour ago. How do you handle it?
24. A manager asks for a "data dump" instead of an analysis. How do you handle this?
25. You have two tasks due at the same time. How do you decide which to do first?
26. A stakeholder is unhappy with the color scheme of your dashboard. How do you react?

### Medium
27. You discover that a key metric in your dashboard has been calculated incorrectly for the last three months. What is your action plan?
28. A department head refuses to share their data because they fear it will make them look bad. How do you gain their trust?
29. During a requirements gathering session, the client keeps adding "nice-to-have" features that will delay the project. How do you manage scope creep?
30. You are presenting data that contradicts the CEO's intuition. How do you deliver the news?
31. The data engineering team tells you that the data you need for a critical KPI won't be available for six months. How do you adapt?
32. A stakeholder changes their mind about the project requirements after you've completed 80% of the work. How do you proceed?
33. You notice a sudden 20% drop in website conversion rates. Walk me through your diagnostic process.

### Difficult
34. Two powerful stakeholders have completely opposite requirements for the same dashboard. How do you facilitate a resolution?
35. You are assigned to a project with extremely poor data quality and no documentation. Where do you start?
36. A project you are on is significantly over budget and behind schedule. How do you communicate the status to stakeholders?
37. You identified a business process that is wasting $100k/year, but the team lead is resistant to change. How do you build a case for improvement?
38. Your analysis shows that a high-profile product launch was actually a failure. How do you present this to the product team?
39. You have been asked to define KPIs for a brand-new business unit with no historical data. What is your approach?
40. A technical limitation prevents you from building the exact dashboard the business requested. How do you offer an alternative that still meets their needs?

## 3. Behavioural Vertical (20 Questions)

### Easy
41. Tell me about a time you had to learn a new tool quickly.
42. Describe a time you worked as part of a team to achieve a goal.
43. How do you handle repetitive tasks in your daily workflow?
44. Give an example of a time you had to explain something technical to a non-technical person.
45. What is your greatest strength as a Business Analyst?
46. Why are you interested in this specific industry?

### Medium
47. Tell me about a time you identified a problem before anyone else did.
48. Describe a situation where you had to persuade a stakeholder who was resistant to your ideas.
49. Give an example of a time you failed to meet a deadline. What did you learn?
50. Tell me about a time you had to handle a difficult stakeholder.
51. Describe a project where you had to manage your time between multiple competing priorities.
52. Tell me about a time you received critical feedback on your work. How did you respond?
53. Describe a time you went above and beyond your job description to help a project succeed.

### Difficult
54. Tell me about a time you had to make a decision without having all the information you needed.
55. Describe a situation where you had to lead a meeting with senior executives. How did you prepare?
56. Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager's decision. How did you handle it?
57. Describe a complex project you managed from start to finish. What were the biggest challenges?
58. Tell me about a time you had to deal with a major conflict within your project team.
59. Describe a time you used data to influence a major strategic shift in your company.
60. What is the most difficult ethical dilemma you've faced in your career as an analyst?
