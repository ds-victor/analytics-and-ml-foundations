# Data Analyst Interview Questions - Comprehensive Pool

This pool contains 60+ questions to help you prepare for Data Analyst roles.

---

## 1. Technical Vertical (20 Questions)

### Easy
1. **SQL:** What is the difference between `WHERE` and `HAVING`?
2. **Pandas:** How do you load a CSV file into a DataFrame?
3. **Data Types:** What is the difference between a 'String' and an 'Integer'?
4. **Visualisation:** Which chart is better for showing parts of a whole (pie chart vs. bar chart)?
5. **Stats:** Define the 'Mean', 'Median', and 'Mode'.
6. **Excel:** What is the purpose of a PivotTable?
7. **Python:** How do you create a list in Python?

### Medium
8. **SQL:** Explain the different types of `JOINs` (Inner, Left, Right, Full).
9. **Pandas:** How do you handle missing values in a DataFrame (e.g., `dropna` vs. `fillna`)?
10. **EDA:** What are the first five steps you take when you receive a new dataset?
11. **Visualisation:** When would you use a 'Heatmap'?
12. **Stats:** What is a 'Standard Deviation' and why does it matter?
13. **Data Cleaning:** How do you handle outliers in a numerical column?
14. **SQL:** What is a 'Subquery' and when would you use one?

### Difficult
15. **Advanced SQL:** Explain 'Window Functions' and provide an example using `RANK()` or `ROW_NUMBER()`.
16. **Pandas:** How would you optimize the memory usage of a large DataFrame?
17. **Stats:** Explain the concept of 'Correlation' and why a high correlation doesn't prove causation.
18. **Visualisation:** How do you design a dashboard for an executive who only has 2 minutes to look at it?
19. **Python:** Write a function that takes a list of numbers and returns only the prime numbers.
20. **Analytics Strategy:** If your conversion rate suddenly drops by 50%, what data points do you check first to find the reason?

## 2. Situational Vertical (20 Questions)

### Easy
21. You are given a dataset with no documentation. How do you start your analysis?
22. A stakeholder asks for a report, but you have no access to the database. What do you do?
23. You find a mistake in your analysis after you've already shared the results. How do you fix it?
24. A manager wants you to use a specific tool that you've never used before. How do you handle it?
25. You have a deadline in two hours and your computer crashes. What is your plan?
26. A user complains that the dashboard is "too slow." What are your first steps to investigate?

### Medium
27. You find that 30% of the values in a critical column are missing. How do you decide between deleting the rows or imputing the data?
28. A stakeholder is convinced that a certain marketing campaign is working, but the data shows it isn't. How do you present this?
29. You are asked to merge data from three different sources with no common ID. How do you approach this problem?
30. Your SQL query is taking 30 minutes to run. How do you go about optimizing it?
31. You notice an extreme outlier that makes the average look much higher than it really is. Do you keep it or remove it?
32. The data engineering team changed the schema without telling you, and all your dashboards are broken. How do you handle the situation?
33. A stakeholder asks for a "drill-down" feature that your BI tool doesn't support. How do you offer a workaround?

### Difficult
34. You are analyzing customer churn and find that the data is heavily biased toward one region. How does this affect your conclusions?
35. You have been asked to build a report for a product that hasn't launched yet. How do you simulate or find proxy data?
36. You identified a major data privacy issue in the raw logs you were given. Who do you tell and what do you do with the data?
37. You have two different data sources giving you two different numbers for the same KPI. How do you reconcile them?
38. A project requires you to use a specific statistical test, but you realize the data doesn't meet the assumptions for that test. What do you do?
39. You are asked to present to the Board of Directors on 10 minutes notice. How do you structure your presentation?
40. You found a way to save the company $1M/year through data analysis, but it requires a department to change their entire workflow. How do you build your case?

## 3. Behavioural Vertical (20 Questions)

### Easy
41. Tell me about a time you had to work with a difficult teammate.
42. Describe a time you were proud of an analysis you did.
43. How do you stay organized when working on multiple projects?
44. What is your favorite data visualization tool and why?
45. Why do you want to be a Data Analyst?
46. How do you handle constructive criticism?

### Medium
47. Tell me about a time you had to explain a complex data concept to someone with no technical background.
48. Describe a situation where you had to manage a project with very little supervision.
49. Give an example of a time you went above and beyond to ensure data accuracy.
50. Tell me about a time you failed to deliver a project on time. What happened and how did you handle it?
51. Describe a time you had to persuade a stakeholder using only data.
52. How do you keep your technical skills up to date in such a fast-moving field?
53. Tell me about a time you had to deal with a major change in project scope.

### Difficult
54. Tell me about a time you found a significant error in someone else's analysis. How did you bring it up?
55. Describe a situation where you had to make a tough decision based on ambiguous data.
56. Tell me about a time you had to lead a data-driven initiative within your team.
57. What is the most complex data problem you've ever solved? Walk me through it.
58. Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager's interpretation of the data.
59. Describe a time you had to balance technical accuracy with a very tight business deadline.
60. What do you think is the most important quality for a Data Analyst to have, and why?
